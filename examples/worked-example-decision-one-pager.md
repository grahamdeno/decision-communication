# Worked Example: Completed Decision One-Pager

This example runs the [Decision One-Pager format](../formats/02-decision-one-pager.md) end to end. The program is a fictional municipal fleet-maintenance modernization. Real structure, invented content.

---

```
DECISION NEEDED: Select the cutover approach for retiring paper work orders
across all five maintenance depots, by 15 Aug.

WHY NOW: The mobile work-order module passes acceptance testing this month.
Winter fleet surge begins 1 Oct, and no depot process changes are permitted
during surge. A decision after 15 Aug forfeits the pre-winter window and
pushes any cutover to spring.

BACKGROUND: Five depots run two-part paper work orders that clerks key into
the system nightly. Reconciliation lags one to three days and the error rate
drives most readiness disputes. The mobile module completed a six-week pilot
at Depot 3 with no data loss and clerk keying eliminated. Training takes one
week per depot. The pilot ran outside surge conditions.

HOW TO JUDGE:
1. Risk to winter surge readiness
2. Speed to citywide data accuracy
3. Training load per depot per month
4. Fallback available if the module fails in field use

OPTION A — Fastest full accuracy (all five depots cut over in September)
  Delivers: citywide data accuracy before winter; one process to manage
  Costs / delays: all training compressed into four weeks; clerks retrain
  during peak leave season
  Risk: a module defect surfacing in October touches the entire fleet
  during the no-fail period, with paper fallback already dismantled

OPTION B — Lowest winter risk (Depots 1 and 3 now, remaining three in spring)
  Delivers: proof at operating scale with paper fallback intact at three
  depots through winter
  Costs / delays: split operations for six months; reconciliation runs two
  processes; full accuracy arrives in spring
  Risk: spring cutover competes with pothole season staffing at the three
  remaining depots

RECOMMENDATION: Option B, because criterion 1 outweighs criterion 2. Winter
surge is the program's stated no-fail period; a fleet-wide defect during
surge is unrecoverable within the season, while the cost of running split
operations is known and bounded.

THE ASK: Approve Option B by 15 Aug.
Silence defaults to no cutover before winter; both options then collapse
into a spring decision and the pre-winter window is lost.

v1.0 | 28 Jul | Prepared by: Delivery Lead, Fleet Modernization
```

---

## What This Example Demonstrates

Every rule from the format, applied:

- **One decision per page.** Cutover approach only. Module scope, training curriculum, and spring sequencing are separate decisions and stay off this page.
- **Options named by what they optimize.** "Fastest full accuracy" versus "Lowest winter risk" tells the reader the trade before a single detail is read.
- **Criteria before options.** The reader knows how to judge before there is anything to judge.
- **Background as a fence.** Six weeks of pilot data reduced to four sentences. Only what the decision requires survives the cut.
- **The recommendation shows its reasoning.** It ties directly back to the criteria and names which criterion won.
- **"Silence defaults to" stated neutrally.** Non-response becomes a visible choice with a factual consequence, not a threat.
- **Dated and versioned.** Because one-pagers get forwarded.
