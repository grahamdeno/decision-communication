# 02 – Decision One-Pager

The workhorse. One decision, one page, readable in ninety seconds, actionable after one read. Use it when the choice doesn't warrant a full options brief, as the cover page when it does, and as the record that travels up the chain after the meeting.

## The Template

```
DECISION NEEDED: [one sentence stating the choice] by [date]

WHY NOW: [the trigger; what makes this decision due]

BACKGROUND: [3-5 sentences maximum; only what the decision requires]

HOW TO JUDGE: [2-4 criteria, before any option]

OPTION A — [named by what it optimizes, not "Option A"]
  Delivers: ...        Costs / delays: ...        Risk: ...
OPTION B — [name]
  Delivers: ...        Costs / delays: ...        Risk: ...

RECOMMENDATION: [option], because [two sentences tying back to the criteria].

THE ASK: Approve [option] by [date].
Silence defaults to [what happens if no decision arrives].
```

## The Rules That Make It Work

- **One decision per page.** Two decisions on one page get one decision made, usually the easier one. Split them.
- **Name options by what they optimize.** "Fastest first payoff" and "Lowest sustainment cost" carry information; "Option A" and "Option B" carry none. The names alone should let a reader guess the trade.
- **The background section is a fence, not a field.** Everything the decision doesn't strictly need goes to an appendix or a link. The discipline of cutting background you worked hard on is what keeps the page at ninety seconds.
- **"Silence defaults to" is not optional.** It converts non-response from a pocket veto into a visible choice. State the default neutrally and factually; it's a planning mechanism, not a threat. Programs with busy stakeholders live or die on this line.
- **Date every version.** One-pagers get forwarded, and stale forwarded versions cause real damage. A visible date and version marker costs one line.

## When It Escalates

If drafting the one-pager surfaces more than four criteria, more than three genuinely viable options, or a background section that refuses to stay short, that's the format telling you this is an [Options Brief](01-options-brief.md). Escalate the format, don't cram the page.
